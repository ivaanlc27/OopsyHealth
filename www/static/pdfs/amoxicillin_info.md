# Amoxicillin — Clinical Summary

## Name: Amoxicillin

### Indication:
- Bacterial infections: otitis media, sinusitis, streptococcal pharyngitis, pneumonia, skin and soft tissue infections, urinary tract infections (depending on local guidelines).

### Dosage (typical adult doses):
- 250–500 mg orally every 8 hours, or 500–875 mg every 12 hours depending on severity.
- Pediatric dosing based on weight; follow guidelines.

### Mechanism of action:
- Beta-lactam antibiotic; inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding penicillin-binding proteins.

### Contraindications:
- Known allergy to amoxicillin/penicillins (type I hypersensitivity).
- Caution in patients with a history of severe penicillin allergy.

### Adverse effects:
- Gastrointestinal upset (nausea, diarrhea), rash; possible antibiotic-associated colitis (C. difficile) in prolonged use.

### Pharmacokinetics:
- Well absorbed orally; excreted primarily by kidneys.

### Notes for clinicians:
- Consider local antibiogram when choosing empiric therapy.
- For some infections combine with clavulanate for beta-lactamase producing organisms (amoxicillin/clavulanate).
