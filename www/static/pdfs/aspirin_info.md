# Aspirin — Clinical Summary

## Name: Aspirin (Acetylsalicylic acid)

### Indication:
- Mild to moderate pain (headache, muscle pain),
- Fever,
- Anti-inflammatory properties in low doses for cardiovascular prophylaxis.

### Dosage:
- Adults: 325–650 mg orally every 4–6 hours as needed (max 4 g/day for analgesia).
- For cardiovascular prophylaxis: commonly 75–100 mg once daily (prescription guidance required).

### Mechanism of action:
- Irreversibly inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, reducing prostaglandin and thromboxane production.

### Contraindications:
- Hypersensitivity to aspirin or other NSAIDs.
- Children/teenagers with viral infection due to risk of Reye syndrome.
- Active peptic ulcer disease, recent gastrointestinal bleeding.
- Severe renal or hepatic impairment (consult physician).

### Adverse effects:
- Gastrointestinal irritation, bleeding, tinnitus at high doses.
- Allergic reactions (aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease).

### Pharmacokinetics:
- Oral absorption; hepatic metabolism; renal excretion.

### Notes for clinicians:
- Avoid combining with anticoagulants without careful risk assessment.
- Use lowest effective dose for the shortest duration necessary.
